Timeline of News and History

Written by:
Dylan Vinesett

Event Setting Description
Invention of the Printing Press 1450, Johannes Gutenburg, Germany Revolutionizes communication via written word and allows the production of printed news media.
First newspaper 1605, Relation aller Fürnemmen und gedenckwürdigen Historien, Germany First to start a very important and still prevalent form of news media
National magazines become prevalent 1830s, US Magazines incorporate entertainment, pop culture, and news into an easy-to-read publication
First news agency founded 1835, Agence France-Presse, France News agencies are important because they compile sources and manpower to create hard news stories as quickly and efficiently as possible
Invention of the radio 1894, Guglielmo Marconi , Italy The radio gave up-to-date news to millions of people around the world
Prevalence of radio broadcast 1920s – 1940s Radio is used for news reporting. Events such as the presidential inauguration are broadcast via radio as early as 1925
First TV transmission 1927, Philo Farnsworth, US Beginning of a long era of television broadcast
Widespread use of television for news broadcast 1950 – present Television is one of the longest running forms of new broadcast. News networks such as CBS and NBC (1947) start broadcasting. They have and continue to make a lasting impact on new broadcast. The first presidential inauguration was in 1947.
First internet newspaper 1980, US The first newspaper was the Columbus Dispatch.
Use of internet for news purposes 1990s – present Internet is arguably the most important advancement for news reporting. The 9/11 attacks are reported almost immediately via internet. Important political events such as presidential debates are streamed on YouTube.
